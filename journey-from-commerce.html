✨ My Journey from Commerce to Code

How I Became a Web Developer

1. Introduction: Where It All Began

When I first chose the commerce stream in high school, I imagined a future in business, finance, or management. It felt like a safe and practical choice, one that many students around me were making too. But deep down, I always had a spark for creativity and a fascination with how technology works. At the time, I didn’t realize that one day, that spark would evolve into a passion for web development.

After completing my HSC in commerce, I went on to pursue a Bachelor’s degree in Computer Applications (BCA). It was during this phase that I truly began to explore the world of tech — not just from a theoretical perspective, but hands-on. What started as curiosity slowly turned into something more meaningful. I was about to embark on a journey that would completely shift my career path — from commerce to code.

2. The Turning Point: Discovering Code

I still remember the moment I stumbled upon a video about how websites are built. It showed someone writing a few lines of HTML and CSS, and instantly turning plain text into a styled webpage. That blew my mind.

Curious, I decided to try it myself. I opened up a basic code editor, typed out a simple HTML file, and styled it with a bit of CSS. When I opened it in my browser and saw my first custom webpage come to life, I felt a sense of joy and accomplishment that I had never experienced with any subject before.

That small experiment opened the doors to an entirely new world for me. I started diving into free resources, tutorials, and YouTube videos. Each new tag, style, and script I learned gave me the confidence to keep going. From there, I began building mini-projects — a to-do list, a simple calculator, even games. I realized that web development wasn't just about writing code — it was a way to bring ideas to life.

3. Learning & Growth

Once I realized how much I enjoyed web development, there was no looking back. I started with the basics — HTML, CSS, and JavaScript — building simple web pages and gradually adding interactivity to them. Each time I hit “refresh” and saw my code come to life, it pushed me to learn more.

I didn’t have a roadmap at first, but I made one for myself. I took online courses, watched tutorials, and practiced by creating real projects. Some of the projects I built include a YouTube Clone, a Modern Landing Page, a Product Card, a Simple Calculator, and a fun Rock Paper Scissors Game. Every project helped me improve my skills and boosted my confidence.

Eventually, I moved on to more advanced concepts like React.js and even started exploring the MERN stack. I also learned Bootstrap to improve my design skills and began working on responsive layouts. I wasn’t just writing code anymore — I was building experiences.

One of the most exciting parts of my journey has been creating my own portfolio website. It's more than just a collection of my work — it’s proof of how far I’ve come.

4. Challenges Faced & Lessons Learned

Switching from commerce to coding wasn’t always smooth. In the beginning, the biggest challenge was simply understanding how programming logic worked. Coming from a non-technical background, terms like loops, arrays, and functions felt like a foreign language. There were days I’d spend hours debugging a small error or getting stuck on a single line of code.

Another challenge was consistency. Learning on your own means staying motivated without exams or deadlines pushing you forward. I had to build discipline — treating coding like a routine, even when progress felt slow.

Imposter syndrome also crept in sometimes. Seeing experienced developers build complex apps made me question my own abilities. But over time, I learned not to compare my Chapter 2 with someone else's Chapter 10.

Each challenge taught me something: patience, problem-solving, and most importantly, that progress is more important than perfection. Every error became a lesson. Every stuck moment eventually led to a breakthrough.

5. Final Thoughts: From Curiosity to Confidence

Looking back, I’m proud of how far I’ve come — from someone who didn’t know the difference between HTML and JavaScript, to someone who can now build websites and interactive projects from scratch.

This journey wasn’t just about learning code. It was about transforming my mindset. I discovered that with consistency, curiosity, and a willingness to learn, I could break into a completely new field — even without a tech background.

I know there’s still a lot to learn, and that excites me. Whether it’s diving deeper into frameworks like React, building full-stack applications, or contributing to real-world projects, I’m ready for what’s next.

What started as curiosity has grown into confidence. And this is just the beginning.
